Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan’s music is a vibrant expression of its multicultural history—an audible legacy shaped by nomadic traditions, Islamic culture, and centuries of exchange along the Silk Road. From the spiritual elegance of Shashmaqam to regional folk styles and epic storytelling performed by bakhshi, music in Uzbekistan serves not just as entertainment, but as a carrier of memory, identity, and social values.

In this project, we turn to digital tools to explore how this musical heritage is structured, connected, and represented in online knowledge spaces. Using SPARQL queries, Wikidata, and large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, we analyze how musical entities—artists, instruments, genres, and traditions—are documented and interlinked. These methods allow us to assess the visibility and completeness of Uzbek music in digital ecosystems, and to reflect on the challenges of modeling rich cultural traditions within structured data and AI outputs.
