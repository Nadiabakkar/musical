üéº Project Topic

This project explores the intersection of cultural heritage and semantic web technologies by focusing on music in Uzbekistan, Morocco, and Tunisia. Through the use of SPARQL queries on Wikidata and zero-shot prompting with language models (ChatGPT, Gemini, DeepSeek), we identify, compare, and validate structured and unstructured knowledge about musicians, instruments, genres, and cultural events. The goal is to highlight existing gaps and propose strategies to enrich Wikidata with underrepresented music-related data from these regions. Our work illustrates how knowledge engineering can contribute to preserving and promoting musical heritage across different cultures.

Music is a universal form of cultural expression that reflects identity, history, and values. It exists in every society but is often underrepresented in structured knowledge bases like Wikidata ‚Äî especially for countries outside the Global North. By focusing on music, we aim to:
  • Showcase the rich musical traditions of Uzbekistan, Morocco, and Tunisia.
  • Identify data gaps and biases in Wikidata's representation of non-Western cultures.
  • Promote cultural diversity in digital knowledge graphs.
  • Use music as a gateway topic that connects language, society, and communication ‚Äî the core themes of our Master‚Äôs program.

üí° Why is it smart academically?

  • Music is multidisciplinary (touches on culture, linguistics, anthropology, data science).
  • It has structured data potential (musicians, genres, instruments, festivals, albums).
  • It fits perfectly with knowledge engineering, which aims to represent human knowledge in machine-readable form.

üß† Why Wikidata?

We chose Wikidata because it is a central hub of structured, open, and multilingual knowledge that powers many other platforms, including Wikipedia, search engines, and AI systems. It allows us to:
  • Work with knowledge graphs, which are crucial in knowledge engineering.
  • Use SPARQL to query data in a precise, semantic way.
  • Contribute to open knowledge, especially for underrepresented regions like Uzbekistan, Morocco, and Tunisia.
  • Connect music data with other entities (people, places, instruments, genres, languages) using linked data principles.

üîç Academic Justification

Wikidata is the largest open knowledge graph in the world.
  • It follows the semantic web standards defined by the W3C (RDF, SPARQL, etc.).
  • It supports multilingualism and global cultural representation.
  • It‚Äôs a real-world tool used in digital humanities, AI, and data science.

üåç Selected Countries and Why?

The countries in our project ‚Äî Uzbekistan, Morocco, and Tunisia ‚Äî were chosen for both personal and academic reasons:

  • These regions are often overlooked in structured data platforms like Wikidata.
  • The comparison across Central Asia (Uzbekistan) and North Africa (Morocco, Tunisia) allows us to explore regional diversity in music knowledge while keeping the focus aligned.
